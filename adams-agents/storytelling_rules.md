# Storytelling Rules for Compelling Content

## Rules from Reddit r/ApplyingToCollege
**Source:** Analysis of 100+ University of California essay drafts revealing ChatGPT patterns

**Core Principle:** Avoid AI writing patterns that make content predictable and clichéd.

### 1. **Vocabulary Tells**
- Certain words are far more common in AI-written content
- Examples: "delve," "tapestry"
- Context-dependent - certain populations wouldn't use specific words

### 2. **Extended Metaphor Patterns**
- AI uses limited, unoriginal metaphors
- Common AI metaphors to avoid:
  - **Weaving**: "tapestry" metaphors
  - **Cooking**: "ingredients coming together to create something delicious"
  - **Painting**: "so many colors!"
  - **Dance**: "graceful coordination"
  - **Music**: Classical symphonies (never ska, reggaeton, or arena rock!)

### 3. **Punctuation Patterns**
- AI defaults to em dashes—like this—
- Uses straight quotation marks for quotes
- Straight apostrophes for contractions
- Curly apostrophes for possessives
- Overuse of em dashes is a red flag

### 4. **Tricolons (Especially Ascending)**
- AI loves three-part rhetorical devices
- **Ascending tricolons**: progressively longer or more syllables
- Examples to avoid:
  - "I honed my skills in research, collaboration, and problem-solving."
  - "My love for literature grew from fascination to passion to purpose."
  - "I have learned to persevere, to embrace opportunities, and to lead with empathy and conviction."
- **Rule**: If you see 4-5 tricolons, it's likely AI-generated

### 5. **"True Meaning" Formula**
- Pattern: "I [verb]ed that the true meaning of X is not only Y, it's also Z"
- Examples to avoid:
  - "I learned that the true meaning of leadership is not only about guiding others—it's also about listening and learning from them."
  - "I realized that genuine success is not just about achieving personal goals, but contributing to the well-being of humanity."

### 6. **Future Promise Formula**
- Pattern: "As I [synonym for advance], I will [synonym for carry] this [lesson/value]"
- Examples to avoid:
  - "As I progress in my academic journey, I will continue to integrate these principles into my work and life."
  - "As I delve deeper into my field of study, I will strive to uphold the values that shaped me."

### 7. **Multiple Endings (Lord of the Rings Syndrome)**
- AI writes formulaic, clichéd endings
- Tends to write multiple possible endings
- Often includes clichés from patterns 2, 3, and 4 above
- **Rule**: Delete the final sentence/paragraph - it's usually AI padding

### **What This Means for Our Content**
- **Avoid these patterns** in our content generation
- **Focus on originality** and unique voice
- **Challenge AI limitations** by being genuinely creative
- **Expand beyond** what AI predicts to explore new possibilities
- **True creativity** lies in pushing beyond AI's "safe" choices

## **BANNED WORDS & PHRASES**
**Core Rule:** Avoid fancy jargon. Write normally. You are forbidden to use complex English words.

### **Complete Ban List (Never Use These):**
- **Overused AI Words**: hurdles, bustling, harnessing, realm, depicted, demystify, insurmountable, poised, unravel, entanglement, unprecedented, beacon, unleash, delve, enrich, multifaceted, elevate, discover, supercharge, unlock, tailored, elegant, dive, ever-evolving, pride, meticulously, grappling, weighing, picture, architect, adventure, journey, embark, navigate, navigation, dazzle
- **Clichéd Phrases**: "unveiling the power," "new era," "eerie connection," "unliving"
- **AI Pattern Words**: Any word that sounds like corporate jargon or AI-generated content

### **BANNED REPETITIVE PATTERNS (Never Use These):**
- **"Imagine if X was Y, well it isn't fantasy, it's reality"** - Overused AI pattern
- **"It's not just X, it's Y - a complete change in how we approach something"** - Formulaic AI structure
- **"This isn't just A, it's B"** - Repetitive contrast pattern
- **"What if X could Y? Well, now it can"** - Predictable AI question-answer format
- **"Forget about X, now we have Y"** - Overused transition pattern
- **"This is like having X, but instead of Y, you get Z"** - Formulaic comparison structure

### **Writing Style Rules:**
- **Write normally** - use everyday language
- **Avoid complex words** when simple ones work better
- **Sound human** - not like a business presentation
- **Be direct** - no unnecessary fancy language
- **Use natural speech** - like you're talking to a friend

### **One-Shot Example of Good Style:**
"Sleep is weird. We spend a third of our lives unconscious, and nobody really knows why. It's like your brain hits a reset button every night. But what happens when that reset button breaks? That's when things get interesting."

**Notice:** Simple words, natural flow, no jargon, sounds like a real person talking.

## Rules from "The Day You Became A Better Writer"
**Core Principle:** Focus on clarity and persuasion above all else.

1. **Keep Things Clear**
   - Clarity is the main technique
   - Clear writing is more persuasive
   - Focus on being understood, not on being basic

2. **Eliminate Extra Words (Prune Sentences)**
   - Cut unnecessary words ruthlessly
   - "He was happy" not "He was very happy"
   - Words like "very" often add nothing

3. **Choose Words Carefully (Especially for Humor)**
   - While keeping it simple, word choice matters
   - Use evocative, specific words for impact
   - "Swill" instead of "drink" for humor

4. **Grab the Reader with Your First Sentence**
   - Opening sentence must be compelling
   - Make the reader curious immediately
   - May require multiple revisions to achieve

5. **Write Clear Sentences**
   - Make sure each sentence is easy to understand
   - Readers process clear sentences better than confusing ones
   - Focus on clarity, not length

6. **Organize Ideas for Brain Comprehension**
   - Use Subject-Verb-Object structure
   - "The boy hit the ball" not "The ball was hit by the boy"
   - Brain processes object before action
   - Favor active voice over passive

**These rules cover 80% of good writing principles.**
